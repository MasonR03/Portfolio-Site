What is a Del Sol?

The Honda CRX Del Sol is a two-door coupe manufactured by Honda from 1992-1997. This small, targa-top coupe was originally meant as the successor to the massively popular CRX from the years before it. The name "Del Sol" means "Of the sun" in Spanish, referring to the removable targa top that can be conveniently stored in the trunk. The Del Sol was sold first in Japanese (JDM) and European (EDM) markets (1992) then was brought to the US (USDM) in 1993.

Special Models

Trans Top

In Japanese and European markets, the optional "Trans Top" was sold. This was an optional power top that could remove the hard targa top or put it back with the push of a button. Click image for video of process!

Isle Green Pearl

During the 1995 model year, a new color was offered. Isle Green Pearl was available only on the VTEC trim of this year. This color was unique to the Del Sol and is highly sought after.

Engine Swaps

The Del Sol is a popular chassis for swapping in a different engine, as it shares the same engine bay and drivetrain as the Civic from that generation. Popular swaps include the B16/18, K20/24, and H20/22 series of engines. These all give a nice boost in power, and are relatively simple to swap in.

Body Conversions

The Del Sol shares many of its drivetrain and frame components with the Civic of the same generation, so it is possible to put certain pieces from the Civic on to the Del Sol, this includes front bumpers, side skirts, etc.
